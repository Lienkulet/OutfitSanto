Our Story

Born on the Corner of
Heaven & the Street

Outfit Santo began as a single idea in a Buenos Aires workshop: that streetwear and the sacred aren't opposites — they're two expressions of the same hunger. The hunger to be seen. To be felt. To matter.

We take the iconography of the divine — angels, crosses, relics, baroque ornament — and we break it open against the raw energy of urban culture. The result is clothing that carries weight. Every stitch is intentional. Every drop is limited.

No fast fashion. No compromise. Just pieces built to last longer than the season that spawned them.

What We Stand For

The Three Pillars

I
Sacred Craft

Every garment is constructed to the standard of something worth keeping. Heavyweight fabrics, woven labels, hand-finished details — built for the long wear, not the quick sale.

II
Limited by Design

We drop in small runs. When a piece is gone, it's gone. Scarcity isn't a marketing trick — it's a commitment to meaning. We'd rather disappoint a queue than flood the world with product.

III
Street Provenance

Our design language comes from the city — from walls, music, ink, and movement. We don't interpret the street from a distance. We live on it. That's where the ideas live too.
